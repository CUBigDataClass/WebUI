Brian McWilliams
About
I am a non-traditional student, returning to CU at 38 in order to complete a second bachelors degree. My first degree in Business Information Systems was a great introduction to computing. As the duties at my job moved into more Software Engineering tasks I found I had gaps in knowledge that were holding me back. I will be finishing my degree in the Spring of 2014.

After graduation I will be returning to work at Spectra Logic, where I have been employed for 13 years. My current team is working on a FreeBSD backend/Ruby on Rails front end for a disk storage appliance designed to pair with our line of tape libraries. I also manage the development lab at Spectra, approximately 200 servers.

In my spare time, I enjoy brewing beer, mead, wine and ciders. I also like to garden and swim.

Role
I am interested in the infrastructure side of projects. I was the point person for interacting with AWS, spinning up and configuring the servers we needed to complete the project.

I also did initial deployments of Hadoop and a single instance Storm "cluster" as our project workflows changed.

Finally, I contributed to some of the web development and integration.

What I learned
I came to this course knowing that Big Data was Hadoop and deploying things to the cloud. I hoped to gain hard skills in those areas. Instead what I found was that there is a LOT more to this area than I thought. If the only tool you have is a hammer then everything begins to look like a nail. Now I know there are many different tools and projects out there for dealing with data at scale, the benifits of using one over the other, and a basic point to start working with them for future work.

During the course of the project, the importance of using some type of Agile process was made clear. Our project morphed not only in what information we were trying to convey, but also in the technologies used. We cycled through Hadoop, Storm, and LSA as major foundations, as well as exporing other spaces along the way. Without the flexibility that Agile gives, the project would have never reached completion.
